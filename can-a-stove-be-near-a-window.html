Can a stove be near a window?

July 2024 · 5 minute read

Answer

If it is a gas stove it should not be positioned next to a window or external door: A draft might blow out the flame or interfere with the cooking; and. Direct sunlight will make the flame extremely difficult to observe.

Simply put, how far does a stove need to be from a window?

Most building rules require a range to be a minimum of 12 inches away from a window for a variety of reasons. A fire on the stove might leap to curtains on the windows. Or a breeze from an open window might blow out the flame of a gas burner and let gas to collect potentially undiscovered before to a possible explosion.
