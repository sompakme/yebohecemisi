Educational background

The seasoned actor went to elementary and high schools in Tennessee. After his elementary education, he joined Science Hill High School. He graduated high school in 1995.

In high school, he played tennis and was a Tennessee state prep tennis singles champion in 1995. His prowess in the sport earned him a college scholarship.

He went to Charleston College, where he captained the men's tennis team. He was an NCAA-ranked player in the Southern Conference.

The actor took one theatre class in college, and his professor encouraged him to change his major to drama, but he did not. In 1999, he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree (honours) in History and Political Science.
