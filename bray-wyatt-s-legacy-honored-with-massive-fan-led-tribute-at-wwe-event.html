Bray Wyatts Legacy Honored with Massive Fan-Led Tribute at WWE Event

September 2024 · 2 minute read

Bray Wyatt was taken from this world far too soon. The Eater of Worlds passed away after he suffered a heart attack, but his memory is still very much alive within the hearts of WWE fans.

Bray Wyatt’s legacy remains strong in WWE and with the fans, despite his unfortunate passing in August. He was known for his fireflies and they returned in a big way during a recent WWE fan event.

During the recent WWE live event in Edinburgh, TX, LA Knight and Solo Sikoa stepped into the ring for their Texas Street Fight. As soon as the match began, WWE dimmed the arena lights, prompting fans to illuminate the venue with their phones as a tribute to Wyatt.

The video of this touching moment has gone viral, and many fans are also dropping their own Bray Wyatt tributes. We will have to see how WWE pays tribute to Bray Wyatt in the future, but it seems that fans are all about celebrating his amazing memory.

Ringside News was told by a tenured member of the creative team that Triple H “is always open to ideas from the writers, and there are always ideas regarding different talents.” That being said, Triple H has not shut down hearing ideas that would bring Uncle Howdy back to television. Only time will tell how that would be received by fans.

You can check out the video below, at around the 1 hour 8 minute marker. The crowd pulled out their cell phones and put those flashlights on for all to see. It was also quite a tribute by those fans during the show.
