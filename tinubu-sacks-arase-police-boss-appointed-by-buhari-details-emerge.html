Tinubu Sacks Arase, Police Boss Appointed by Buhari, Details Emerge

October 2024 · 3 minute read
  • President Bola Tinubu has announced the appointment of Hashimu Argungu as the new chairman of the PSC
  • Argungu's appointment means the termination of the appointment of Solomon Arase, who was appointed by former president Muhammadu Buhari in January 2023
  • The new appointee retired as a deputy police commissioner in 2016 and is the current chairman of NPF Micro-finance Bank

Legit.ng journalist Bada Yusuf is an accomplished politics and current affairs editor, boasting over seven years of experience in journalism and writing.

FCT, Abuja - President Bola Tinubu has sacked Solomon Arase as the chairman of the Police Service Commission (PSC).

Arase, a former Inspector General of Police, was appointed by as the PSC chairman by ex-president Muhammadu Buhari in 2023.

Shortly after the termination of his appointment, Hashimu Argungu, a deputy Inspector-General of Police, was named the new PSC chairman.

Tinubu appoints Argungu as chairman of PSC

The presidency announced this in a statement signed by Ajuri Ngelale, presidential spokesperson, on Monday, June 10.

The statement partly reads:

“President Tinubu appoints new leadership for Police Service Commission and Nigerian Police Trust Fund.”

Tinubu also appointed Chief Onyemuche Nnamani and DIG Taiwo Lakanu as secretary and member of the PSC respectively.

The appointment of Argungu and others is subject to confirmation by the senate.

Tinubu appoints Sheidu as NPTF executive secretary

In the same vein, Tinubu appointed Mohammed Sheidu as the new executive secretary of the Nigeria Police Trust Fund (NPTF).

According to a statement, the appointment took immediate effect.

Argungu is currently the chairman of the NPF Micro-finance Bank. He will turn 67 on July 10.
